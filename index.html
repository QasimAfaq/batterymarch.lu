  • While the AIFM does not actively take investment decisions based on sustainability risks or consider the adverse impacts of sustainability risks, as part of its due diligence and investment decision-making process, it does not invest or invests limitedly in certain sectors or companies whose products, services or activities could be considered contrary to the current trends regarding the promotion of sustainability criteria. Some sectors are completely excluded from investment on environmental, social or governance grounds.

  • The AIFM recognizes the importance of incorporating sustainability principles and the adverse impacts of investment decisions. Due to the size, nature, and scale of its activities, the AIFM does not currently consider the principal adverse impacts of its investment decisions on sustainability factors. However, the AIFM will continue to monitor developments in this area and will reassess its approach as the regulatory framework evolves and practical experience increases. The AIFM is committed to reviewing its practices and considering the principal adverse impacts when the regulatory framework and market practices provide greater clarity and practicality."

  • As a de-minimis AIFM, the AIFM is not required to have a formal remuneration policy.
